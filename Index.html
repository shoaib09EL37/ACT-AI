Physics Reference Panel

Resistivity Equation:
$R = \rho \frac{l}{A}$

Where $R$ is Resistance ($\Omega$), $\rho$ is Specific Resistance ($\Omega\cdot\text{m}$), $l$ is conductor length ($\text{m}$), and $A$ is cross-sectional Area ($\text{m}^2$).

Temperature Variation:
$R_t = R_0 (1 + \alpha_0 t)$

Where $\alpha_0$ is temperature coefficient ($/^{\circ}\text{C}$) at reference point $0^{\circ}\text{C}$. For standard Copper, $\alpha_0 = 1/234.5 \approx 0.00427/^{\circ}\text{C}$.

Electron Drift Velocity:
$v_d = \frac{I}{n \cdot e \cdot A}$

Where $n$ is carrier concentration, $e$ is electronic charge ($1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}$), and $v_d$ is the very slow axial drift speed of free carriers.
